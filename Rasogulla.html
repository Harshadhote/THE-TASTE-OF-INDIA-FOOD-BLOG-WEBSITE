Rasgulla Recipe

Enjoy the sweet and spongy Bengali delicacy, Rasgulla, with this simple recipe.

Ingredients:

  • 1 liter full cream milk
  • 2-3 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon rose water (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare Chhena: Bring milk to a boil in a large pan. Add lemon juice gradually and stir continuously until the milk curdles. Turn off the heat and let it sit for a few minutes.
  2. Strain Chhena: Strain the curdled milk using a muslin cloth or a fine strainer to separate the chhena (paneer) from the whey. Rinse the chhena under cold water to remove the lemon flavor. Squeeze out the excess water.
  3. Knead Chhena: Knead the chhena for 7-10 minutes to make a smooth, soft dough. Divide the dough into small equal-sized balls, ensuring there are no cracks.
  4. Prepare Sugar Syrup: In a large pan, mix sugar and water and bring it to a boil. Stir until the sugar dissolves completely. Add rose water if using.
  5. Cook Rasgullas: Gently add the chhena balls to the boiling sugar syrup. Cover and cook on medium heat for about 15-20 minutes. The rasgullas will double in size and become spongy. Ensure there is enough space in the pan for the rasgullas to expand.
  6. Cool and Serve: Allow the rasgullas to cool in the syrup. Refrigerate for a few hours before serving. Enjoy chilled rasgullas as a delightful dessert.
